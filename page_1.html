Faults & Fixes

Become an Independent Golfer

The whole reason you go to a coach is to learn and improve your swing. But one of the biggest complaints from average golfers is that their range game doesn't transfer to the course.

Why? Because they don't show up to practice with an independent golfer mindset. That means holding yourself accountable between sessions — learning to self-assess from two key camera angles.

📸
Face On

Camera faces your chest. Used to assess posture, alignment, and arm path through impact.

Setup tip: Chest height, hands centred in the frame — imagine an X from corner to corner, your hands at the intersection.
🎥
Down the Line

Camera sits behind you, pointing toward the target. Best for tracking swing plane and club path.

Setup tip: Same height rule — hands centred in the X. Align the camera down your target line.

When you're taking lessons, ask your coach exactly what to look for in solo practice. Work on one thing at a time. That single focus is what lets the range game finally show up on the course.

When you can recognise a mishit the moment it happens — because you've watched enough video and talked through adjustments with your coach — that's when you've earned the right to call yourself an independent golfer.
